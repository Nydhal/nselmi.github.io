Emergent Impossible Fractal Geometry

A 4-State Cellular Automaton on Triangular Tessellations

A one-dimensional cellular automaton with 78 transition rules over a 7-cell neighborhood whose space-time evolution, rendered on a triangular tessellation, produces structures perceived as impossible 3D fractal objects under isometric projection. The system extends Wolfram's classical connection between elementary automata and fractal geometry—exemplified by Rule 90 generating the Sierpinski triangle—into a regime where the emergent fractals inhabit impossible space. Among its outputs is the Selmi triangle, a fractal Penrose tribar with self-similar structure at every scale, first described in 2009.
